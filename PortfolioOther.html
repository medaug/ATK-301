About

I have been involved in the arts for as long as I can remember. Growing up, my grandmother taught me the basics of music and singing. As a retired performer, she really opened my eyes to the creative world and taught me that anything is possible in this life. I grew up on the voices of Judy Garland, Ella Fitzgerald, Frank Sinatra, and Dean Martin. My young interest in music pushed me into the curiosity of performance. I began performing in choir around the age of 6, got involved in voice lessons, and eventually joined my school band in the fifth grade. I continued my creative career into middle school, still in band and choir, and then starring in two musical productions- "Annie" and Disney's "The Little Mermaid." I found my true voice during this time, pushing me to go farther into my music career in highschool. In highschool I played clarinet and tenor saxophone for our Symphonic and Jazz bands. Traveling across the country to perform, I found my true passion singing with the Jazz Band. That experience pushed me to really pursue a creative career. I now still enjoy playing instruments and singing, and am able to go after a degree that still allows me to have a creative outlet in the work place. While a lot of careers in the arts can be frowned upon, I knew what I wanted to pursue, and have been willing to do the work to get there ever since. I hope that by visiting my page, you take something away from it. Wether it be inspiration for you to follow you passion or if you simply need me to create a website or mix a song for you, I really hope you enjoyed your look around my page!
